Climbing is a unique sport, it does not appeal to the average person and that is mostly because it is inherently dangerous. Do not attempt to climb any of the rocks that you see without expert guidance.

El Capitan is the pinnacle of most climbers' achievments

Climbing is one of my passions. I discovered it while hikihg through Yosemite with a friend. He decided to make a pit stop on our way home to check out some rocks in Tuolumne Meadows and I immediately fell in love. It combines everything I love into one sport; strength, endurance, precision and technique. All of this and you get to see some of the most beautiful places while doing it. Each style of climbing requires a unique blend of skills and strength. Bouldering, speed and sport climbing were some of the recent additions to the olympics.


There are so many incredible places to climb in the world...

It all started with a horde of hippies who essentialy squatted in Yosemite national park, calling it their home for an extended period of time. They were later referred to as the Stonemasters.

Ever since the Stonemasters, the sport of climbing has progressed at a breakneck pace. There are new grades, or difficulties, being discovered every year. What's most impressive to me is how young some of the strongest climbers in the world are.
